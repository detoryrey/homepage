Map Scadrial

The people here live under the Lord Ruler, an immortal king that presides over all known lands. Long ago, the Lord Ruler defeated a great evil and ascended to godhood, becoming the Sliver of Infinity. He remade the world as he saw fit, elevating those who supported him while casting out those who opposed him. From the many cultures of Scadrial he formed a hierarchy that persists to this day, crushing all efforts of rebellion or dissent under his incredible might.

Luthadel

Home to the Lord Ruler, its structures are Gothic in style, and it is considered the most fortified and powerful city on Scadrial. This is, of course, thanks to the Lord Ruler. Surrounding the city, within its walls, are gates placed at regular intervals, each crafted from a primary metal: copper, brass, pewter, zinc, and others. Like all major cities in the Final Empire, Luthadel was built near a number of mines, thus hampering the ability of Ruin to monitor the establishment of the Lord Ruler's stockpiles.

Mistborn

Allomantic Metal

Allomantic Metals are substances used to fuel Allomancy, a power that allows users (Mistborn or Mistings) to gain various physical, mental, and temporal abilities by "burning" the ingested metal, which acts as a conduit for power. There are 16 base metals, often grouped into Internal/External and Push/Pull categories within four broader groups: Physical, Mental, Temporal, and Enhancement. Burning a metal causes a warm feeling in the stomach and vaporizes the metal, making it a consumable resource.

Wizarding World

The wizarding world, also referred to as the magical community, was the society in which wizards and witches lived and interacted, separate from non-wizarding society. The two communities were kept separate through the use of charms, spells, and secrecy. Wizards were forbidden to reveal anything about magic to Muggle society due to the International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy. Each country had a form of wizarding government to oversee magical affairs in their territory, such as a Ministry of Magic or a Council of Magic. The International Confederation of Wizards served as a wizarding intergovernmental organisation.

Harry Potter

Marauders map

The Marauder's Map was a magical document that revealed all of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Not only did it show every classroom, every hallway, and every corner of the castle, but it also showed every inch of the grounds, as well as all the secret passages that were hidden within its walls and the location of every person in the grounds, portrayed by a dot. It was also capable of accurately identifying each person, and was not fooled by Animagi, Polyjuice Potions, or Invisibility Cloaks; even the Hogwarts ghosts or Peeves were not exempt. Nor were animals, such as the caretaker's cat.

Harry Potter

Hogwarts founders

The Hogwarts founders, also known as the Hogwarts Four, were a quartet of witches and wizards who established Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry during the medieval era, in the 10th century. These four founders, Godric Gryffindor, Helga Hufflepuff, Rowena Ravenclaw, and Salazar Slytherin, were widely described as being the most brilliant witches and wizards of the time. Each of them created their own House in Hogwarts: Gryffindor, Hufflepuff, Ravenclaw, and Slytherin respectively. Students were divided up into these Houses based on the virtues that each of the founders valued most in their students. They laid the foundations for the largest and most prestigious Wizarding school in Britain.

A Song of Ice and Fire

King's Landing

King's Landing is the capital of the Seven Kingdoms, located in the crownlands on the east coast of Westeros. It is situated along the kingsroad at the mouth of the Blackwater Rush and overlooks Blackwater Bay. King's Landing is the site of the Iron Throne and the Red Keep, which is the seat of the king, Robert I from House Baratheon of King's Landing. The main city is surrounded by a wall, manned by the City Watch of King's Landing, also known as the gold cloaks. King's Landing is the most populous city in Westeros, but unsightly and dirty compared to other cities.

A Song of Ice and Fire

Valyria Map

Valyria, also called Old Valyria, is a ruined city in Essos. It is a long-dead city of wonderment, and was once the capital of a great empire called the Valyrian Freehold. It was destroyed by a cataclysmic event known as the Doom of Valyria a century before Aegon's Landing. It is the ancestral home of House Targaryen, House Celtigar, and House Velaryon. Valyria was on the Valyrian peninsula, which extended into the Summer Sea. The Doom of Valyria shattered the peninsula, separating the city of Valyria from the Lands of the Long Summer by the Smoking Sea.

A Song of Ice and Fire

Roshar Map

The world is Roshar. The map shows a time period of great prosperity in the world, and as the story unfolds, you begin to understand the changes and degeneration that has occurred throughout the centuries. The climate, terrain, flora, and fauna are all unique. Roshar is commonly besieged by highstorms that always blow east to west. These storms are violent and tumultuous, and they contain enough force to blow large boulders through the air. Because of these common storms, most of the land has no topsoil, and the plant life has evolved to become quite hearty and in some ways mobile. Grasses and vines are able to react to stimulus and retreat into the earth when threatened, and other plants, such as tress, can fold their branches. Additionally, the animal life is mostly crustacean based. Giant crablike creatures, called chulls, pull wagons as would a horse/ox/donkey on Earth. People keep axehounds as pets, which are like weird alien hybrids of a dog and a crustacean. Like the plant life, the animals have evolved hearty shells to protect against the highstorms. Another unique element is the seasons are unpredictable and do not follow a regular pattern like on Earth. For example, Roshar may get two weeks of winter followed by three weeks of spring, and then a few weeks of autumn. It appears that the seasons shift at random and only last a few weeks typically.

The Stormlight Archives

Warcamp

A warcamp is the domain of an Alethi highprince, his retainers, his soldiers, and his bridgemen on the Shattered Plains. Each of the ten warcamps is enormous, and all told, there are over a hundred thousand troops in the various Alethi camps. This number doesn't count the civilians present. (A mobile camp attracts camp followers; stationary camps like these on the Shattered Plains bring even more.) Each warcamp fills its own crater* and is filled with an incongruous mix of Soulcast buildings, shanties and tents. Even within the crater, the stormwinds can be strong, particularly where the outer wall is low or broken. Some places inside the wall remain completely exposed. Soldiers from one camp didn't mix with those of another, and one stayed away from another Brightlord's crater unless there was business to be done there. Whorehouses are common in every camp. Shallan thinks that the warcamp craters must. Dalinar's warcamp is laid out with blocks of barracks forming a large circle around the lighteyed buildings closer to the center. The market is in the outer ring of camp followers beyond the soldiers. Everything there is arranged so that as many doorways as possible can point away from the Origin of Storms, streets designed so that they can run east to west and provide a way for winds to blow.

The Stormlight Archives

Stormseat Map

Stormseat was the capital of Natanatan. It was destroyed during Aharietiam, or the Last Desolation, but this occurred so long ago that nobody talks about just how it happened. Something more terrible than a fire or an earthquake broke the city - shattered it, literally. The ruins of the city are at the center of the Shattered Plains in the Frostlands. It is still connected by Oathgate to Urithiru, and has the only known functioning Oathgate on-world. At some point, Stormseat was destroyed by the same event that transformed the Natan interior into the Shattered Plains. There is some disagreement as to when this happened. Human sources place the destruction of Stormseat at Aharietiam, the last of the old Desolations. Listeners, however, put it significantly earlier, as according to their stories, by the time the Last Legion was sent into the area, the Shattered Plains already existed. In truth, the plains were Shattered by a brief clash between Honor and Odium. Following the destruction, Stormseat wasn't rebuilt, but rather left to crumble. Over time, the nation of Natanatan fell apart, until only a single coastal city, New Natanan, claims descendancy from it. It is perhaps for this reason that when Urithiru was abandoned prior to the Recreance and all the other Oathgates were locked from both sides, the one in Stormseat remained accessible.

The Stormlight Archives

Power

Power is the in-universe term used for Magic Systems in Powerhouse Universe. Here, the forces that shape the world are not mere tools but the core of identity and heritage, a presence so integral to the nations of this realm that it is simply termed Power. Each kingdom within this boundless cosmos is graced with its own distinct manifestation of Power, a signature force that defines its culture, its populace, and its stance in the ever-evolving tapestry of the realms. These Powers vary as widely as the lands that hold them, from the tempestuous energies harnessed by warriors in the throes of battle to the delicate, complex threads that can mend flesh, spirit, or reveal the hidden truths of the universe. The pride each nation holds in their Power is a vehement blaze, alight with the belief in their ascendancy over their neighbors. In this realm of disparate strengths, the gift of Power is not bestowed equally. In some territories, the force is a precious gem, granted only to a select few who rise to prominence and power. In others, Power permeates the air, a common right shared by all, binding the community together. Beyond the confines of these nations, in the untamed expanses known as the Wastelands, dwell those who transcend the traditional limits of Power. These are the Abnormals, beings whose very essence is a confluence of forces, drawn from the myriad realms.
